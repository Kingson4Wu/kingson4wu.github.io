# Article Types

Use the closest type as scaffolding. Do not copy these skeletons verbatim.

## Technical Summary

For architecture, language, database, networking, AI, distributed systems, or tool-learning posts.

Recommended structure:

```markdown
[Lead: what problem/topic triggered this article and why it matters now]

## 背景

[Where this concept appears in real work]

## 核心理解

[Mechanism, model, or key distinction]

## 实际使用中的注意点

[Tradeoffs, pitfalls, scenario boundaries]

## 小结

[When to use it, when to be careful, author's judgment]
```

Rules:

- Prefer practical explanation over exhaustive definition.
- Introduce concepts only as needed for the author's point.
- Explain tradeoffs with specific scenarios.
- Keep diagrams, commands, code, and links when useful.

## Troubleshooting / Postmortem

For incident records, bug diagnosis, socket leaks, performance problems, or operational failures.

Recommended structure:

```markdown
[Conclusion first: what happened and what caused it]

## 现象

[Observable symptoms]

## 排查过程

[Hypotheses, checks, commands, dead ends]

## 根因

[The actual mechanism]

## 修复

[What changed and why it worked]

## 复盘

[What to check earlier next time]
```

Rules:

- Keep the diagnostic trail honest.
- Preserve commands and evidence.
- Avoid dramatizing the incident.
- Name false fixes or misleading clues when present.

## Workplace Reflection

For management, meetings, communication, replaceability, team work, and career judgment.

Recommended structure:

```markdown
[Concrete workplace situation or observed phenomenon]

## 现象

[What happened or what is commonly seen]

## 为什么会这样

[Incentives, organization, communication, or human reasons]

## 我的判断

[Direct stance with boundaries]

## 结语

[What the author will do, avoid, or accept]
```

Rules:

- Keep judgment explicit.
- Do not smooth everything into "both sides are reasonable".
- Avoid pure emotional venting; connect criticism to cost, incentives, or work quality.
- Keep the author's directness, but make the prose controlled.

## Reading Note / Idea Note

For books, articles, videos, or external ideas.

Recommended structure:

```markdown
[What was read and why it triggered this note]

## 让我印象深刻的地方

[The idea, paraphrased or briefly quoted]

## 我的理解

[How the author connects it to work, life, technology, or past beliefs]

## 保留的疑问

[Optional: disagreement or uncertainty]

## 小结

[What this idea changes or reinforces]
```

Rules:

- The author's reaction matters more than summary completeness.
- Reduce long quote piles.
- Keep selected quotes only when they support the author's own thinking.

## AI / LLM Technical Observation

For AI tools, LLM concepts, agents, RAG, prompt engineering, skills, and developer workflow posts.

Recommended structure:

```markdown
[Concrete AI usage, concept, or surprise]

## 先说结论

[Main judgment in plain language]

## 我是怎么理解的

[Mechanism or mental model]

## 对开发工作的影响

[What changes in workflow, capability, taste, architecture, or learning]

## 需要警惕的地方

[Limits, hallucination, over-trust, evaluation, maintenance cost]

## 小结

[How the author will use or watch this technology]
```

Rules:

- Remove hype and generic future-talk.
- Keep curiosity and surprise, but ground them in actual use.
- Avoid empty "embrace change" endings unless tied to a specific practice.
