# Style Profile

Use this as the stable voice target.

## Core Identity

- Pragmatic, direct, and judgment-heavy.
- Writes like a frontline engineer who cares about real work, not polished abstraction.
- Values correctness, efficiency, boundaries, reliability, and useful experience.
- Often connects technical judgment with human or organizational judgment.
- Prefers recording what was learned over performing expertise.

## Voice Feel

- Clear, not ornate.
- Personal, not self-indulgent.
- Reasoned, not artificially neutral.
- Sometimes sharp, but grounded in a concrete situation.
- Professional after revision, but still recognizably written by a real person.

## Stable Traits

- Start from a concrete case, work problem, reading trigger, or personal experience.
- Move from phenomenon to mechanism to practical judgment.
- Distinguish ideal situations from real-world constraints.
- Care about scenario boundaries: when something works, when it does not, and what it costs.
- Prefer "this is how I understand it" over universal proclamation.
- Use examples from architecture, backend development, networking, distributed systems, team work, and AI tools.

## Common Language Tendencies

Preserve the feel of these tendencies without forcing the exact words:

- 简单总结一下
- 这里做一个记录
- 从实际工作来看
- 本质上
- 核心问题是
- 实际上 / 其实
- 个人理解
- 不一定完全正确
- 取决于具体场景
- 需要注意的是
- 更准确地说

## Article Shape

The strongest target structure is:

1. Concrete trigger: why this is being written.
2. Background or observed problem.
3. Mechanism, process, or decomposition.
4. Tradeoffs, boundaries, or mistakes.
5. Practical judgment or grounded closing.

## Keep

- Real work context.
- Direct conclusions.
- Specific engineering constraints.
- Personal judgment and mild sharpness.
- The habit of recording learning while it is fresh.

## Improve

- Convert scattered notes into article-shaped prose.
- Strengthen heading hierarchy.
- Add transitions where old posts jump between points.
- Replace generic AI prose with concrete experience and reasoning.
- End with the author's actual judgment, not a broad slogan.
